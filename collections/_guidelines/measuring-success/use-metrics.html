In order to determine the success of a project or product, it is important to make use of quantifiable metrics, especially in modern (i.e. internet) environments that allow development teams to easily collect data that tells them whether their projects are fulfilling their objectives.

What to measure

While external metrics (i.e. those set by management) should be considered, it is useful for the team to develop internal (i.e. self-defined) metrics too. Particularly, factors that are meaningful and relevant to users should be identified as metric KPIs.

Customer satisfaction, adoption rate and conversion are some examples of widely-used metrics.

Government development teams can make use of WOGAA, a web analytics tool for Government officers to gather usage statistics and patterns for their digital services easily, at no cost.

At the same time, statistical digital analytics should not be the sole metric measure. A range of data sources should be used, such as the cost of hosting transactions, call centre data or qualitative feedback like user satisfaction feedbacked through interviews.

Developing OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) is useful for the frequent setting, tracking and re-evaluating of objectives and key results. This ensures all team members are heading in the same direction, with clear priorities, in a constant rhythm to achieve targeted goals. If a project falls short of achieving the goals of the project or is unsustainable in terms of money or time, consider retiring it. Learn from mistakes and if possible, reuse previous technologies for improved proficiency in new projects.
