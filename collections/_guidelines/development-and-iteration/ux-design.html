Overview

The purpose of user experience design is to take responsibility for all aspects of the end-users’ interaction with government services and its associated digital experiences. It starts with the gathering of user insights, its translation into the design and implementation of user interactions, content, interfaces and processes, but can stretch to reimagining entire service journeys to make them simpler, more human-centred and more impactful. This means:

  • Designing how people will interact with a digital product or service, and how to elicit the desired user behaviours and outcomes

  • Defining how information should be structured, the relationships between different types of content and making it easy to navigate

  • Defining how the presentation layers of a product or service look, to elevate the user experience, usability and engagement

  • Running usability testing to identify friction, inform design iteration and identify improvements

  • Making sure everyone can use the digital service by testing for accessibility and addressing usability challenges that might lead to people being unintentionally excluded

UX design is about supporting the agile development of the digital product or service in a sustainable way, and continuing to iterate and make improvements informed by user research, testing and product data. It should not be an exercise to simply deliver on user stories.

UX design domains

UX designers typically leverage on a broad collection of design skills and experiences that together support the development of great user experiences. In reality, no two UX designers are exactly alike - there are design-generalists who have a broad base of competencies; others are design-specialists with a depth of expertise in select competency areas; some excel at craft and individual contribution, others excel at leading multi-disciplinary product teams and stakeholder engagement.

Roles also differ due to team size. On smaller teams, one UX designer is usually sufficient to cover all bases - a design-generalist who can cater to all potential UX needs. Larger teams with more resources may divide their design team into more specific roles. They might distinguish between design researchers, interaction designers, content strategists and service designers. Bigger organisations may also have more variation in terms of leadership skills and domain expertise.

In assembling the product team, it worth spending the time to think through and articulate the specific design skills and deliverables required and the phase of the project lifecycle the project is currently in (discovery, product conceptualisation, agile development, etc.)

The whole team, and in particular the designers, user researchers, content designers and developers, must work together to design, build and iterate a service based on user needs

Specialist Roles

  • Design Researcher

  • Interaction Designer

  • Content Strategist / UX Writer

  • Service Designer

Design Researcher

Performs primary and secondary research to inform the design of a product, process or service journey by understanding user behaviours, needs and goals.

  • Deep expertise in user interviews and usability testing; and breadth in other relevant research methodologies

  • Translates research data into insights, stories, clear findings that inform product decisions

  • Identifies measures (and ways of tracking proposed measures) to indicate user experience success

  • Leads the synthesis and documentation of research to translate individual research project findings into broader learnings and principles for the product team

  • Defines knowledge gaps and strategic opportunities for research to inform the future direction of the product

Interaction Designer

Defines how people will interact with a digital product or service, and how to elicit the desired user behaviour, responses, flows and outcomes.

  • Develop end-to-end interaction flow diagrams that address specific user requirements (and identify alternate interaction pathways for edge and error cases)

  • Implement consistent interaction patterns according to principles set to elicit desired user behaviour

  • Develop UI designs that are intuitive to use

  • Set up, run and interpret usability testing, communicate findings, and prioritise design iterations

  • Implement visual standards set by product or organisation guidelines

  • Working knowledge of motion design principles and tools

Content Strategist / UX Writer

Defines how information should be structured, the relationships between different types of content and making it easy to navigate across a product or service.

  • Develops users’ mental models for organising information and identify ways to make complex information and processes easy to understand

  • Implements information architecture based on data, user needs and stakeholder priorities

  • Writes concise, well-crafted messages that supports and brings to life the content strategy of a product or service

  • Develops content consistently, maintaining voice and style across a product

  • Implements usability tests on content and identify content gaps between current and desired level of user experience

  • Uses content modelling to optimise content for improved user experiences

Service Designer

Creates and delivers a seamless service experience that meets the needs of users through the craft of tying together human, digital, and physical interactions, over time, across a journey (in conjunction with policy, process, digital enablers)

  • Defines a service journey vision, strategy and a roadmap to implementation (including key measures of success and how we could track them)

  • Deep expertise in facilitation of the design thinking/service design process; to identify opportunities for transformation (and some breadth in other relevant design disciplines)

  • Translates user needs into service journey goals, opportunities, experiences and measures of success

  • Ability to develop and test service experience prototypes of different levels of fidelity

  • Develops detailed service experience documentation that inspires and guides change
