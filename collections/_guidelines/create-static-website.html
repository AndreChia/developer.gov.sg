Overview

A static informational website typically only consists of HTML, CSS and JavaScript files to be served to clients, such as a desktop or mobile web browser. It is delivered to a user exactly the way it's stored. Since a server-side technology is not required to dynamically generate content, static websites are relatively straightforward to build and deploy.

Isomer - Static Website Generator

Isomer is a static site generator that allows government agencies to build static information websites that comply to the Digital Service Standards (DSS) easily.

Isomer is an informational website generator powered by Markdown using GitHub. Isomer is free of charge and will cover hosting, maintenance, support and training for the system.

To learn more, email admin@isomer.gov.sg with your name, agency, and information about the website you want to build or migrate.

Digital Service Standards

All public facing digital services of the Government must meet the Digital Service Standards (DSS).

Digital Service Standards (DSS) guide agencies to implement their digital services to meet the Digital Government Blueprint (DGB) goal of delivering digital services that are easy, seamless and relevant for our citizens and businesses.

The Government conducts mystery-shopping checks based on the DSS, to ensure that our digital services are implemented consistently that are easy, seamless and relevant for our citizens and businesses.

Singapore Government Design System

The Singapore Government Design System consists of guidelines and UI components implemented through a CSS-based client-side library. It enables teams to easily build websites that are accessible and compliant. SGDS is developed and maintain by GovTech.

Design System is a collection of reusable component, guided by clear standards, that can be assembled together to build any number of applications that comply to Digital Service Standards.
