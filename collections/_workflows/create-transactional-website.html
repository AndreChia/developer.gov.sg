Overview

Whether you are building a static information, dynamic information or transactional website, we have tools to support your development.

Transactional Website

Transactional websites provide users with the ability to conduct transactions through the financial institution's website by initiating banking transactions or buying products and services.

Transactional websites typically enable the electronic exchange of confidential user information and the transfer of funds, services provided through these websites expose a financial institution to higher risk than static and dynamic informational websites.

Wholesale e-banking systems typically expose financial institutions to the highest risk per transaction, since commercial transactions usually involve larger dollar amounts. In addition to the risk issues associated with informational websites, examiners reviewing transactional e-banking services should consider the following issues:

  • Security controls for safeguarding user information
  • Authentication processes necessary to initially verify the identity of new users and authenticate existing users who access e-banking services
  • Liability for unauthorized transactions
  • Losses from fraud if the institution fails to verify the identity of individuals or businesses applying for new accounts or credit on-line
  • Possible violations of laws or regulations pertaining to consumer privacy, anti-money laundering, anti-terrorism, or the content, timing, or delivery of required consumer disclosures
  • Negative public perception, user dissatisfaction, and potential liability resulting from failure to process third-party payments as directed or within specified time frames, lack of availability of on-line services, or unauthorized access to confidential user information during transmission or storage

Customisation

Transactional websites can apply UI Components from the Singapore Government Design System, developed and maintain by GovTech.

Design System is a collection of reusable component, guided by clear standards, that can be assembled together to build any number of applications that comply to Digital Service Standards.
