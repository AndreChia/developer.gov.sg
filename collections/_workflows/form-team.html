Overview

Agile is more of a mindset based on the values and principles of the Agile Manifesto. Scrum is an Agile framework that provides a guide to implementing an Agile way of working in an organisation.

At GovTech, we use Scrum to help our cross-functional teams work together effectively. Scrum is based on empiricism, which states that you should make decisions based on what you know and what you know comes from experience. That’s why our approach is iterative and incremental. So that we can make decisions based on results, rather than speculate or guess. For Scrum to work, there must be transparency, inspection and adaptation.

A scrum team consists of a product owner, a scrum master and the development team. The scrum team is self-organising and self-managing, which means they decide how best to deliver their work, rather than being directed by someone outside the team. They are also cross-functional – all the skills or competencies required to do the work are within the team.

The Product Owner

The product owner should be someone with vision, authority and availability. The product owner needs to have a vision for how the product should develop and the authority to make decisions. They are also responsible for communicating the vision and priorities to the development team.

The product owner ensures the team delivers maximum business value by prioritising and managing the product backlog.

The product owner is responsible for:

  • Writing the product backlog items clearly
  • Prioritising the product backlog items to achieve the most value and to meet the team’s goals
  • Making sure the product backlog is visible, transparent, shows the team what to work on next and that the team understands the items

If the product owner is to succeed, the organisation needs to respect their decisions and anyone wanting to change the priority of a product backlog item must go through the product owner. No one else should give the development team a different set of requirements.

The Scrum Master

The scrum master ensures that Scrum is understood and practiced by the product owner, the development team and the organisation.

The scrum master does this by ensuring the scrum team follows Scrum theory, practices and rules, and removing any obstacles the team encounters.

The scrum master is responsible for:

  • Facilitating daily scrum, sprint planning, sprint demo and retrospective meetings
  • Needs to forecast the numbers of deliverables possible in an iteration, which is based on evidence and reliable source
  • Needs to be an expert in estimation and planning and knows how to create useful, reliable and practical plans for software development projects
  • Ability to track and remove impediments
  • Acts as safeguard for his team

If the product owner is to succeed, the organisation needs to respect their decisions and anyone wanting to change the priority of a product backlog item must go through the product owner. No one else should give the development team a different set of requirements.

The Development Team

The development team is responsible for building the product or service. Usually there are about 7–8 people in the development team, which includes software engineers, quality engineers, architects, designers, business analysts and any other skills required to deliver the work. Ideally, the development team sits in the same room and are completely dedicated to the project.

While each individual team member might have an area of specialisation or focus, the whole team is responsible and accountable for the work done. In order for the development team to be accountable, the organisation needs to empower them to organise and manage their own work.

Here are some characteristics of a Development Team:

  • Team members share the same norm and rules
  • It is empowered and working as autonomous as it is possible
  • The skills within the team are balanced
  • It is small and has no sub-teams

If the product owner is to succeed, the organisation needs to respect their decisions and anyone wanting to change the priority of a product backlog item must go through the product owner. No one else should give the development team a different set of requirements.
