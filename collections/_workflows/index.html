Introduction

You will understand Agile Software Development Life Cycle, within the context of our efforts on the delivery of transformative digital projects in Singapore Government.


Introduction

The agile software development lifecycle is dominated by the iterative process. Each iteration results in the next piece of the software development puzzle.

Concept Phase

You collaborate with stakeholders to consider this potential project and invest in enough effort to decide whether to consider funding the effort further.


Product Conceptualisation

The purpose of discovery is to identify and understand the problem that needs to be solved, before committing to building a digital product or service.

Digital Government Blueprint

DGB is a statement of the Government’s ambition to better leverage data and harness new technologies, and to drive broader efforts to build a digital economy and digital society, in support of Smart Nation.

Minstry Family Digitalisation Plan

MFDP comprises suggested approach, key principles and other reference resources that will serve as a guide to relevant officers involved in developing digitalisation plans for their agencies.

Inception Phase

You identify with your team the complexity of the problem space, technical complexity and ability of stakeholders to come to agreement as to the scope.


Form Cross-Functional Team

A scrum team consists of a product owner, a scrum master and the development team. The scrum team is self-organising and self-managing, which means they decide how best to deliver their work.

Identify What You Will Need

The decisions we make must enable the team to work efficiently and allow deployed services to scale easily and cost-effectively.

Identify Product Features

The product backlog is a prioritised list of all the work a development team needs to deliver in future releases of the product.

Construction and Iteration

You incrementally deliver high-quality working software which meets the changing needs of our stakeholders.


Create a Static Website

A static information website is the most basic kind of website, and the easiest to create. It requires no server-side processing, only client-side.

Create a Dynamic Website

Dynamic sites are blogs, e-commerce sites, calendar or to-do sites, or any site that needs to updated often.

Create a Transactional Website

Transactional websites provide users with the ability to conduct transactions through the financial institution's website by initiating banking transactions or buying products and services.

Release Phase

You will need to adopt a continuous deployment strategy to automate many of the technical aspects to transit your system into production.


Ensure Production Readiness

Production readiness is the practice of ensuring that your software is continuously in a state where it could be shipped at a moment’s notice.

Deploy the Solution

In software deployment an environment or tier is a system in which a software or component is deployed and executed.

Production Phase

You need to keep the system running and help users to use it.
