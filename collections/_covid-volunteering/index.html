COVID-19 Idea Sprint - Build & Ideate with Us!

Launching in conjunction with GovTech’s Hack the Crisis: Crowdsourcing ideas for digital solutions to curb the spread of COVID-19 webinar held on 8 May, GovTech is hosting a COVID-19 Idea Sprint between 8 - 17 May to crowdsource ideas and prototypes in order to address other COVID-19 related challenges faced across the Singapore Government. 

Amongst other problem statements to be shared in future webinars, we will be looking for ideas on the following topics addressed in the Hack the Crisis Webinar:

  1. The Government is finding ways to quantify the benefits and costs to lessening circuit breaker measures and opening the economy. We would like to hear volunteers’ suggestions on models or optimisations (with parameters) that could guide policymakers in making decisions.

  2. How might we improve the contact tracing process to identify patterns in linked / unlinked cases so that we may generate the best quarantine yield possible in the shortest time and with the smallest manpower required through the use of technology and data?

    For the purpose of this problem statement, a close contact is defined as a person who has been in close proximity with an infected case within 2 metres and sustained for 30 minutes or more.

Additional problem statements and webinar sessions will be shared next week on the COVID-19 Idea Sprint page. Go to https://go.gov.sg/covid19-ideasprint to take part.
