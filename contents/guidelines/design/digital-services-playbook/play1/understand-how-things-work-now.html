Research

UX is not something new. It’s a shift in mindset that places people at the centre of the design process.

User Experience is an experience that encompasses all aspects of the end-user’s interaction with the company, its services, and its products. - Nielsen Norman Group

This means that designing a positive experience requires looking further beyond what you are designing for. Your service may just be one step of a user’s multi-step workflow involving other tools or services.

A UX practitioner aims to understand how people use a product or service and what their motivations are, so he or she can develop better solutions. People enjoy a positive experience when it is easy and quick for them to fulfill their needs.

A good place to start is to study how people use your product or service and reduce the amount of work they need to do.

Simply put, UX is about designing something that’s both useful and usable. To get there, you need to research, build and test over and over again.

Ecosystem Map

It is unlikely that your new solution will be born in a vacuum. Map out the various existing digital touchpoints your business has with your users, and consider how your potential solution can fit into the ecosystem.

You may also discover existing resources that you can leverage and extend or improve on.
