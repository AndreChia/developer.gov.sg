Overview

Production readiness is the practice of ensuring that your software is continuously in a state where it could be shipped at a moment’s notice.

It’s an attitude and a mindset in which the team treats the first iteration of their software as something that could potentially be shipped, and each iteration thereafter as simply updates to a live production system.

Benefits

We can reduce a lot of risk by acting as if we had to release it every week.


Faster Development

Once you get into the rhythm of regularly producing working software you spend less time:

  • fixing bugs
  • deploying emergency patches
  • rewriting broken features

While having more time to:

  • improve user experience
  • adding (or removing) new features

Easier Project Planning

Features are either done or they are not. It removes any doubt with regard to where you are with the project, and how much you have left to do.


Reinforces the Good

To regularly produce shippable code each week there are certain things that your team is doing:

  • Breaking big problems into smaller and manageable ones
  • Testing plan from the start
  • Receiving feedback from users

Getting Started

Teams that regularly produce production-ready software usually have some combination of the following three things.


Discipline

It is always temping to take the easy way out. Discipline is what separates you from everyone else.


Culture of Excellence

Set the bar high in the beginning and keeping it there for the duration of your project:

  • Do not accept untested, poorly implemented features
  • Do not accept people abusing the build
  • Do not accept bugs

Care about your Project

Attract and hire people with this mindset for quality and craftmanship, because when everyone care about what they are doing:

  • You do not need a lot of rules
  • You do not need to write everything down

This is because good people do not need to be told what to do. They take initiative and do the right thing naturally.
